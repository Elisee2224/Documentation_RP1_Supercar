Task:

You are Tom Fig, an employee at a medium sized company, named Lion Fields Ltd. Your company is considering implementing a four-day workweek policy to increase employee satisfaction and productivity. You have been asked by your teammates to write an email to your manager, proposing the idea of implementing a four-day workweek policy and highlighting its potential benefits for the company.

You should include the following points:

· Reasons for implementing a four-day workweek policy

· What would this policy comprise of?

· How would this policy benefit the company?

· Why is the current five-day workweek not beneficial for the employees and the company? The email address of your manager is as follows: richardmars@lionfltd.ac.mu. You have to write an email of about 250 words.
Answer
Subject: Proposal for Implementing a Four-Day Workweek Policy
Dear Richard,
I hope this email finds you well. I am writing to propose an idea that I believe could significantly enhance employee satisfaction and productivity at Lion Fields Ltd.
I suggest that we consider implementing a four-day workweek policy, where employees work four days a week instead of the current five-day workweek. This policy would involve reducing the standard workweek from 40 hours to 32 hours, while maintaining the same pay.
The four-day workweek policy has several potential benefits for the company. Firstly, research has shown that employees who work four days a week are more productive and engaged than those who work five days a week. This is because they have more time to rest and recharge, which leads to higher levels of motivation and energy.
Secondly, a shorter workweek could help attract and retain top talent. In today's competitive job market, employees are looking for employers who offer flexible work arrangements and a better work-life balance. By offering a four-day workweek, we can differentiate ourselves from other companies and make ourselves a more attractive employer.
Thirdly, a shorter workweek could reduce absenteeism and turnover. When employees are overworked and stressed, they are more likely to take sick days or look for new jobs. By giving them more time off, we can reduce absenteeism and retain our best employees.
Finally, a shorter workweek could help reduce our carbon footprint. By reducing the number of days that employees commute to work, we can lower our greenhouse gas emissions and contribute to a more sustainable future.
While the five-day workweek has been the standard for many years, it is not without its drawbacks. Employees who work five days a week often feel overworked and stressed, which can lead to burnout and decreased productivity. Moreover, the five-day workweek can be inflexible and may not accommodate the needs of employees who have families or other responsibilities outside of work.
In conclusion, I believe that implementing a four-day workweek policy could be a game-changer for Lion Fields Ltd. It would not only benefit our employees but also our company as a whole. I would be happy to discuss this proposal further and provide more information on the potential benefits and logistics of implementing such a policy.
Thank you for your time and consideration.
Best regards,
Tom Fig
